01 · The Story

A modern voice for a discipline built on trust.

Kirtane & Pandit has built its reputation across decades of audit, assurance, tax and advisory work. Our identity reflects that heritage — confident, considered, precise — while making room for the modern, technology-enabled practice we are today. This document captures the visual and verbal system that holds it all together.

Promise

From the first conversation to the final certificate, KPCA promises clear thinking, clear communication, and decisions you can stand behind.

02 · Logo System

One mark. Four lockups. No exceptions.

The KPCA logo is the wordmark KIRTANE & PANDIT set in a refined navy serif, with a single italic red ampersand as its visual signature. It appears in four official lockups depending on context. Always use a supplied master file — never re-typeset.

Primary Lockups

Clear Space & Minimum Size

Maintain clear space equal to the height of the capital "K" on all sides. Never place text, edges, or other graphic elements within this zone.

Minimum size — Print

28 mm wide

Master lockup with "Chartered Accountants" tagline legible.

Minimum size — Digital

120 px wide

Below this, switch to the Wordmark-only lockup.

Logo Don'ts

✓Approved master lockup, full color
✕Don't skew, stretch or distort
✕Don't recolor outside the system
✕Don't place on busy or branded artwork
✕Don't add shadows, glows, outlines
✕Don't use the logo on red backgrounds
Pairing rule: the logo and the brand graphic elements (Section 05) should not appear directly adjacent or overlapping. Treat them as separate citizens in a layout — the logo identifies, the graphic device decorates.
03 · Color

Two colors do the heavy lifting.

Navy carries gravity, longevity and trust. The single red ampersand carries our character. Together they account for >90% of the brand's visual weight in any document. A small secondary palette exists for the brand's graphic elements and for moments of emphasis — but it never leads.

Primary Palette

Use Navy as the dominant color in any layout. Use Red sparingly — typically as a single accent element (rule, eyebrow, ampersand, marker), not a fill.

05 · Brand Elements

A geometric device that signals KPCA.

The brand element is a composition of overlapping isometric panels — solid filled planes layered with transparent outline planes — built from the brand palette. Each composition reads as an abstract architectural mark, suggesting structure, depth and the layered nature of professional work. Four compositions are official; each comes in four color variants.

The Device

Layered planes,
quiet authority.

Each composition contains 3–5 sheared rectangles at a fixed isometric angle. Solid fills carry the dominant color; outline planes echo the same forms in a complementary hue. The asymmetry is deliberate — never let a composition appear perfectly balanced or mirrored.

Use them for: report covers, section dividers, hero visuals, social cards, presentation titles. Don't use them for: backgrounds behind running text, watermarks behind tables, or as repeating patterns.

The Four Compositions

Each composition is named for its silhouette. Use the same composition consistently across a related set of documents (e.g. all covers of a report series).

06 · Voice & Tone

Senior, plain-spoken, never showy.

Whether the audience is an audit committee, a regulator, a CFO, or a new joiner — KPCA writes the same way. We sound like the most experienced person in the room: confident enough not to puff, considered enough not to over-promise.

PRINCIPLE 01

Lead with the conclusion.

Open with what we found, recommend or believe. Reasoning follows. Don't make readers wade to the bottom for the answer.

PRINCIPLE 02

Plain words for hard ideas.

Technical accuracy is the floor, not the ceiling. If a sentence works without a Latin phrase, drop it. Jargon is permitted only when there's no plain equivalent.

PRINCIPLE 03

Be precise about what we're not saying.

Spell out scope, assumptions and limits. Saying "this opinion does not cover X" is a hallmark of senior practice, not a weakness.

PRINCIPLE 04

One promise, no superlatives.

"World-class", "best-in-class", "cutting-edge" — these are out. State what we do; let the work speak.

PRINCIPLE 05

Active voice, short sentences.

"We reviewed the controls and found three weaknesses" beats "Controls were reviewed and certain weaknesses were identified." Average sentence length: under 22 words.

PRINCIPLE 06

Numbers stay precise.

Round only when rounding is the point. Indian numerals (lakh, crore) for India-facing work; international format when the audience is global. Be consistent within a document.

Sound like KPCA

Do — KPCA voice

We've audited the consolidated financial statements. In our opinion, they present fairly, in all material respects, the financial position of the Group as at 31 March 2026.

The control gap in revenue recognition is significant but addressable. We've outlined three steps the team can take this quarter.

Technology has changed how audits are done. We use it where it gives clients better answers, faster — not for its own sake.

Don't — Off-brand voice

We are pleased to provide our valued client with a comprehensive and bespoke audit solution leveraging cutting-edge methodologies and world-class expertise.

Significant material weaknesses have been identified within the revenue recognition control environment which may necessitate remediation.

As a forward-thinking, future-ready partner of choice, we harness AI-driven, cloud-native, end-to-end digital transformation paradigms.
